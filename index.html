About Kain Pelikat

Kain Pelikat is a traditional checkered sarong widely worn in Malaysia, Indonesia, and Singapore. Known for its bold patterns and lightweight cotton fabric, it is a staple of comfort and cultural identity.

History of Kain Pelikat

See how this sarong changed over time:

1400s-1700s

First came from India with traders. Sailors wore it for work.

1800s

Local makers added Malay patterns and colors.

Early 1900s

Became everyday clothing for many families.

Today

Still worn traditionally, now also used in modern fashion.

Why Kain Pelikat Matters

How people use this special sarong:

For Prayers

Muslims wear it during prayer because it's simple and respectful.

At Home

People love wearing it at home - it's cool and comfortable.

For Special Days

Worn at weddings and festivals to show Malay culture.

Master the Art of Wearing

Learn the proper techniques to wear your Kain Pelikat with confidence and style.

1

Prepare the Fabric

Unfold completely and hold horizontally behind you at waist level, ensuring equal length on both sides.

2

Wrap Around

Bring both ends to the front, crossing them slightly at your waist for a secure fit.

3

Secure the Fold

Roll the top edge down about 2-3 inches to create a tight, comfortable waistband that stays in place.
